Structure

The Heart is a powerful muscle. It is normally the size of your fist. Through regular contractions, the heart pumps blood throughout the body. The human heart is made up of four chambers; two aria and two ventricles.


The right atrium and right ventricle recieves blood into the heart, then pumps the blood to the lungs. After picking up Oxygen the blood flows to the left atrium and left ventricle. The left atrium and ventricle send the blood out to the body.
